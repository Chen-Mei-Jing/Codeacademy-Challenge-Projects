On Myanmar's Mystery Peak, Drama and a Challenge Like No Other

ONE MORNING IN early November, three highly skilled alpinists inched their way along a spine-like ridge, whipped by 70-mile-per-hour winds. They had clawed their way up this precipice, hoping to reach the summit of an obscure mountain in northern Myanmar (Burma), and in the process settle a long-standing geographic question: What is Southeast Asia's highest mountain?
